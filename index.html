03 — Top 3 I'd Test First

If I were bringing these to Blank Street, I wouldn't launch all nine at once. Good product thinking means knowing what to prioritise — and why. Here are the three I'd run first, and the reasoning behind each.

1
Matcha Yuzu Tonic
Easiest to scale, broad demographic appeal, low prep complexity. The sparkling format opens matcha to a wider audience who might not usually order it. Low risk, high reward.
Easiest to scale
2
Elderberry Yogurt Matcha
Premium feel without being intimidating. The yogurt cream is batchable, the jam can be prepped in advance. It sits at the sweet spot between accessible and elevated — perfect for Blank Street's positioning.
Premium but approachable

Cartoon Cat Matcha
Built-in viral potential. One good photo and this drink markets itself. It builds barista engagement, drives social sharing, and creates a reason for customers to come back and try again. Social ROI is huge.

04 — If I Were Building These for Blank Street

Designing drinks for a growing café brand is not just about creativity. It is about balancing flavor, speed, consistency, and customer excitement. If I were testing these concepts at Blank Street, I would approach them as small experiments rather than final menu items.

🧪
Customer Testing
  • Run limited pop-up trials or seasonal releases before committing to the menu
  • Gather quick feedback on sweetness, texture, and flavor clarity from real customers
  • Track which drinks customers reorder — reorder rate is the real signal
⚡
Operational Simplicity
  • Ensure all ingredients are easy to prep in batches — no single-order components
  • Limit build complexity so drinks remain fast during peak morning hours
  • Prioritise components that can be reused across multiple drinks on the menu
⚖️
Menu Balance
  • Maintain a mix of familiar and adventurous flavors — not everything should be challenging
  • Include one visually fun drink (like Cartoon Cat) that encourages social sharing
  • Keep the menu aligned with Blank Street's approachable, joyful style at every price point
05 — Why Blank Street

Blank Street represents something I care deeply about: bringing high-quality drinks to everyday routines while keeping the experience approachable and joyful.

Over the past two years I've been building a small matcha research project of my own. What started as curiosity turned into a genuine obsession — testing blends from different producers, developing recipes, and running small pop-ups to understand how people actually experience matcha drinks in real life.

What excites me most about Blank Street is the balance between craft and scalability. The challenge of designing drinks that are both exciting and operationally consistent across many stores is something I find genuinely fascinating — and something I'd love to learn more about from the inside.
